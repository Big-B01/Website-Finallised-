We’re Gonna Make It

Written: September 2025

                        My childhood friend and I
                couldn’t be more different.
                Yet, to this day,
                I call this man my brother.

                I’m the elder,
                but somehow, he’s lived twice the life I have.
                He drinks the night away,
                smokes the ghosts out,
                and walks out with the prettiest girl in the club.
                I just shake my head
                that’s just his gravity.

                I remember a night on his balcony.
                Nothing special:
                a supermarket, a few flats,
                the kind of view nobody writes about.
                But for us it was an empire.

                He lit a cigarette,
                smoke curling into the dark,
                and we talked the way we always had.
                Then he said it
                words that felt like they fell from the sky:
                “I’m gonna make it.”

                You might wonder why that matters.
                But hear me, he meant it.
                This is a man who broke his foot,
                got hit by a bus,
                and walked away with nothing but scratches.
                I pushed him through the supermarket in a wheelchair,
                and he still grinned,
                still whispered it like a prayer:
                “I’m gonna make it.”

                He’s fought battles I’ll never know.
                Carried pain in the kind of silence
                reserved for boys raised not to cry.
                And I realized then,
                even as the older one, I was jealous.
                Jealous of his fire, his stride,
                his refusal to let go
                while I was busy looking for the exit,
                ready to leave the world behind.

                But I get it now.
                Those words weren't meant to make me small.
                They were a battle cry.
                And he was right.

                Because no matter the day,
                no matter the wreckage,
                or how the seasons turn
                it won’t just be him,
                and it won’t just be me.
                It will be we.

                I’m talking the "you and me" kind.
                We’re gonna make it.

A Love from Afar

Written: March 2025

                                    July 13, 2021 you said yes.
                                    The world shifted, and I was sixteen,
                                    naive enough to believe that distance
                                    was just a number, not a canyon.
                                    My friends called me foolish,
                                    but they didn’t know the hum of my phone
                                    felt like a heartbeat.

                                    We fell into a jagged rhythm:
                                    Mondays were for promises,
                                    but by Saturday night, the air grew thin.
                                    I learned to say sorry for sins I didn’t commit,
                                    buying peace with apologies
                                    because losing you felt like losing everything.

                                    I gave you flowers for anniversaries,
                                    a dog to call our own,
                                    a faithful heart that never wandered.
                                    But your jealousy was a slow infection.
                                    My world got smaller
                                    deleted friends, monitored screens,
                                    an exhausting audit of my loyalty.
                                    I was trying to build a home
                                    while you were busy building a cage.

                                    Then came Morocco.
                                    The summer sun hit differently there.
                                    Among new faces, I found a reflection
                                    of who we used to be easy, light, and loud.
                                    And in that heat, the truth finally cooled:
                                    I had spent years fighting for us,
                                    but you never once fought for me.

                                    So, when you finally suggested the end,
                                    I didn’t chase the car.
                                    I didn’t flood the screen with blue bubbles
                                    or beg for a hearing.
                                    For the first time, the silence didn’t feel like a void
                                    it felt like room to breathe.

                                    Now you return every month like a season,
                                    flooding my inbox with "what ifs" and "could haves."
                                    But I wonder where that fire was
                                    when my hands were blistered from holding on.

                                    You were once my entire map.
                                    Now, you are just an echo in a room I’ve left behind.
                                    I’ve finally learned my worth,
                                    and it’s more than a ghost of a girl
                                    who only loves me when I’m gone.

Loyalty Isn’t Loud

Written: August 2024

         We walked toward the metro through the evening glow,
                the girl I liked beside me, moving soft and slow.
                Her eyes were on her phone until she caught a line,
                my bio staring back “Value loyalty above all else” that line.

                She looked at me with wonder, a spark inside her eyes,
                “Why do you care so much about loyalty?” came her reply.
                I smiled instead of speaking, let the question hang in air,
                then led her through the station doors without a single care.

                We found two seats together as the train began to sway,
                city lights blurred past us in a silver grey display.
                She nudged me with her shoulder, “Come on, tell me true 
                why does loyalty matter so much to you?”

                Instead of giving answers made of words and clever sound,
                I took her phone and dialed, then gently placed it down.
                “Say you need some help,” I whispered, “tell him I sent you.
                Just trust me for a moment I’ll show you what is true.”

                The phone rang twice, a voice answered kind and clear,
                “Hello? Who is this calling?” he spoke without fear.
                “Hi, I’m Cammy,” she said, voice unsure and light,
                “Brian told me to call you… I think I need some help tonight.”

                The man didn’t pause, didn’t ask her who she was,
                didn’t wonder why a stranger called him just because.
                “Because Brian’s always had my back, no questions, no demands,
                if he sent you, then I’ve got you that’s just how loyalty stands.”

                She thanked him softly, hung up, and stared down at the screen,
                something shifted in her eyes, a quiet understanding seen.
                The rails kept humming underneath us like a beating heart,
                as I finally spoke the truth that set her question apart.

                “Loyalty isn’t loud, love. It doesn’t need applause.
                It lives in hidden actions and unspoken sacred laws.
                It’s the hand that reaches quickly when no one else can see,
                the quiet debt we pay forward that’s what loyalty means to me.”

                She didn’t speak for minutes, just watched the darkness fly,
                then slowly laid her head upon my shoulder with a sigh.
                The train kept rushing forward through the tunnels cold and deep,
                while two hearts rode in silence no more words we needed to speak.

                In that quiet rumble, something beautiful was shown:
                loyalty isn’t always loud sometimes it’s only known
                by how it moves in secret, by how it shows its face,
                in metro lights and stolen time, in actions over grace.

Even If You Never Love Me

Written: 2026

                        I know that it ain’t your fault you don’t feel the same,
                it ain’t your fault that I fell so hard,
                it ain’t your fault that my heart never listened
                to the warnings and barriers my brain put up.
                But why did you make me feel so safe,
                why look at me with those eyes
                that I could never say no to?

                Now the nights feel heavier without your voice in them,
                now I lie here replaying every single look you gave,
                now my chest keeps tightening at the smallest reminders
                even though I keep telling myself this was never promised.
                I still don’t blame you for not feeling it back,
                but I still don’t understand why you held me like that,
                why your touch made all my fears go quiet for a while,
                only to leave me louder than I was before you came.

                I introduced you to my friends, to what I do after school.
                I put you on a pedestal so high that I fear even I can’t reach it anymore.
                Every moment with you felt like a blessing,
                but now that I need to forget you, I can’t seem to let you go.
                I see you everywhere at every party, every break, every game.
                I tried so hard to forget about the idea of you.
                So when I got quiet you came to comfort me,
                you patted my head, asked for a hug and what was wrong.
                How could I possibly tell you that you were the problem
                when all I wanted to do was hug you in that moment?

                I stood there frozen while your hand touched my head,
                arms open like nothing had changed between us.
                The words burned behind my teeth but never left.
                How do I say you’re the reason I’ve gone quiet
                when your hug still feels like the safest place I know?
                So I lied and said I would fix myself.
                I gave you a side hug at first but it wasn’t enough 
                you wanted a real one.
                So I did, because I can’t seem to stop saying yes to you.

                So we laid down on the couch because you said we needed to talk.
                You wanted to know what was wrong, why I looked so sad.
                But I wasn’t sad I was mad.
                Mad at myself for falling so hard,
                mad for not listening to my brain when it told me to stop,
                mad that I did it all to myself and could never tell you.
                After you kept asking and I said I wouldn’t talk about it,
                you asked if it was about me and you.
                I said no, of course I did.
                I care too much about you to hurt you,
                even if you broke me without knowing it.

                Now a couple of days passed and we were at practice.
                I saw the pain in your eyes because I’ve seen it in mine.
                You were scared for the exams, and before I knew it
                a tear rolled down your face.
                I sat behind you with the side of my body against your back,
                telling you that no matter what, it would be okay.
                Then I stood up and sat right in front of you, grabbing your hands for a second.
                I told you how smart you really are even when I joke that you’re not
                that you work hard and I believed in you.
                I asked what I could do to make it better.
                You thought for a second and said a hug.

                How could I possibly refuse?
                I had wanted to go to a party a friend was throwing afterwards,
                just a little get-together so I could forget about you for a second.
                But how could I, when all I wanted was to make you happy,
                even if that meant you would never end up with me?
                I would do anything for you, so I told you that if you wanted, you could come with.
                We drank wine, ate snacks, and played fun little mini games.
                Seeing you smile was all I ever wanted,
                even if it broke me inside, piece by piece.
                That’s the way I’m wired
                I know you would never love me.

                So I smiled anyway, watched you shine while something inside me quietly died.
                I kept choosing your laughter over my own peace,
                kept being the safe place you could run to
                even as I bled in silence.
                Maybe this is all I was ever meant to be for you 
                the giant who holds you gently,
                the fool who loves without conditions,
                the boy who will always say yes
                even when it destroys him.
                And when the night finally ends and I walk home alone,
                I’ll carry this heavy, stupid heart with me,
                still hoping you find everything you deserve…
                even if that everything was never me.

More precious than gold

Written: May 2026

                 I miss my friend.
                    Not in the way you miss the dead,
                    but in the way you miss laughter echoing down hallways
                    that no longer belong to us.

                    I miss the version of us that didn’t need plans.
                    Breaks between classes,
                    talking shit like it was oxygen,
                    playing stupid games, eating worse food,
                    two idiots convinced the whole world was funny
                    as long as we were in it together.

                    This week I opened an old assignment
                    and found the audio project we made.
                    I had a stupid crush on that girl back then,
                    so of course I dragged him into it
                    made him help me write a fake dialogue about The Bellflower.
                    Two years we were glued like that.
                    Where I was, he was. Where he was, I was.

                    I pressed play.

                    And his voice came through the speakers…
                    Man, I wasn’t ready.
                    That same stupid laugh. That same rhythm when he talks.
                    I didn’t know missing someone could hit you this hard
                    when they’re still alive and only a few messages away.

                    Now I walk through uni and see ghosts wearing his hoodie,
                    same height, same walk.
                    Sometimes I run up and hug them before my brain catches up,
                    apologizing with a awkward laugh.
                    They don’t understand.
                    How could they?

                    I have new people now good people, loud people, beautiful people.
                    But some memories refuse to be replaced.
                    The camping trips.
                    The cafés we haunted until they knew our orders.
                    The 4 a.m. calls that meant nothing and everything.
                    The way we built stupid little projects like they were monuments.

                    It might sound small to someone else,
                    but those days are more precious than gold to me.
                    Not because they were perfect
                    but because we were stupid, young, and fully there.
                    Fully together.

                    And even if we never get those exact moments back,
                    I keep them locked inside every time I smile at an old voice note,
                    every time I hug a stranger who walks like my brother,
                    every time I hear the word loyalty and remember
                    what it actually sounded like
                    laughing next to me on a cracked couch at 2 a.m.
