Internship Observation Days

Business Maker Module – Evidence

About This Experience

Documentation from my internship observation days. Each photo captures a real moment and a lesson from my time on the job. Click any photo to view it full size.

Working on the Road

Internship doesn't always happen behind a desk. Here I was in the passenger seat, taking notes, planning the next move, and staying productive while we traveled between locations. I learned that real business runs on flexibility — your office is wherever your laptop opens.

Purchasing Stock

One of my responsibilities was helping source and select new stock. From scanning shelves to negotiating prices, I got hands-on experience in supply and inventory decisions. It taught me how every product on a shelf is a business decision — what to buy, how much, and at what cost.

Working Dinner

Not every important meeting happens in a boardroom. Over dinner, we discussed strategy, brainstormed new ideas, and reflected on the day's progress. I learned that some of the best business conversations happen when everyone's relaxed and the food is good.

Building the Audience

We took a group photo to share on Facebook — content that would help us grow our likes, engagement, and reach. This was my first real lesson in how content marketing isn't just about posting; it's about telling a story your audience wants to be part of.

Working Late at Night

Long after the office would normally close, we were still at it — laptops open, ideas flowing. These late-night sessions showed me what dedication really looks like. When you care about the work, the clock stops mattering.

On the Road to Germany

Cross-border business is a different beast. Driving to Germany taught me about logistics, planning, and the small details that make international work happen. The view from the window was a constant reminder: opportunity is always one road trip away.
